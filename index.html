Step 2 · Climb

The L0 → L3 ladder

Each level answers a sharper question than the last. Every card is a service you can add onto a deployed Landing Zone. Click one to open the inspector — switch lenses for an Executive, Architect, or Practitioner view, with copy-ready snippets.

0
L0 · Govern & Land

Foundation & guardrails

"Is the platform ready to be observed safely?"

Before a single metric flows, give observability a governed home: a dedicated compartment, least-privilege IAM, consistent tags, secrets in Vault, and tenancy-wide Audit. This is the layer that bolts directly onto your OCI Landing Zone.

1
L1 · See & Alert

Foundation telemetry

"Is it healthy — and what just happened?"

Turn on the three native pillars — Monitoring, Logging, Audit — plus the routing layer of Notifications and Events. Actionable alarms only, severity by business impact, the right alert to the right channel.

2
L2 · Diagnose Deep

Database & analysis depth

"Why did it happen, and are we running out of room?"

Make databases a first-class domain: Database Management for live diagnosis, Ops Insights for capacity and forecasting, Log Analytics for root-cause work, and Management Agent for hybrid reach.

3
L3 · Correlate & Automate

End-to-end & proactive

"What's the business impact — and what can the platform fix itself?"

Stitch traces, logs, metrics and database signals together with APM + OpenTelemetry, move data with Connector Hub, automate via Events, and layer on AI-assisted operations and forecasting.

Step 3 · Attach

Every service is a Landing Zone add-on

Your OCI Landing Zone gives you the secure foundation — compartments, networking, identity, guardrails. Observability is not a separate build: each service here is designed to drop in after the Landing Zone is deployed, inherit its compartments and tags, and start producing signal.

  1. Deploy the Landing Zone — or point at your existing one. Core identity, network and security guardrails come first.
  2. Add the L0 observability foundation — the observability compartment, IAM groups, tag defaults and Vault scope extend the LZ topology.
  3. Bolt on services level by level — Monitoring, Logging, DB Management, APM and the rest enable per compartment or per resource, no teardown.
  4. Iterate by use case — use the finder to add only what each pattern needs, then climb toward L3.
